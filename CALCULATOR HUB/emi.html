About EMI

Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is the fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal every month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

EMI depends on three factors: the loan amount, the interest rate, and the tenure. With an EMI calculator, users can easily estimate their monthly payments for different types of loans such as personal loans, home loans, and car loans.

This allows borrowers to plan their finances better and make informed decisions while choosing a suitable loan product that aligns with their financial goals and capabilities.

What is EMI?

EMI refers to the fixed amount you pay monthly towards your loan. It includes both principal and interest. This tool helps you:

  • Budget effectively: Understand your financial commitment.
  • Compare loan options: Find a repayment option that fits your budget.
  • Make informed decisions: Plan ahead with clarity.

EMI Calculation Formula

EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1]

Where P = Principal, R = Monthly interest rate, N = Loan tenure in months.
